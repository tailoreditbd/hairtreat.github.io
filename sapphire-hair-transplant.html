FAQ about Sapphire hair transplant

Sapphire hair transplant is an advanced FUE method where sapphire blades are used to create small, precise recipient channels before implanting hair grafts. It is used for natural-looking hairline and density planning.

Sapphire FUE may be suitable if you have stable hair loss, enough donor hair, and realistic expectations. A doctor consultation is needed to confirm graft requirement, technique, cost, and recovery plan.

The procedure usually takes several hours in one day, depending on the number of grafts. Some patients may need additional time for consultation, preparation, and initial follow-up.

Sapphire hair transplant is usually performed under local anesthesia, so major pain is not expected during the procedure. Mild discomfort, swelling, or sensitivity may happen during recovery.

Cost depends on graft requirement, hair loss stage, donor quality, procedure complexity, and treatment package. A consultation gives the most accurate Sapphire hair transplant cost idea.

Initial shedding can happen after transplant. New growth commonly starts gradually over the following months, with fuller visible improvement usually developing over 9 to 12 months depending on individual healing.
